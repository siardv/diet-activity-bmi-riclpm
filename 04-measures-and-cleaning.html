S4 · Measures and cleaning

This vignette records every measurement and cleaning decision, including the construction stage between the raw modules and the merged panel; the audit quantifying that stage is scripts/05_bmi_construction_audit.R (make audit), and its outputs render in S6.

Focal constructs

BMI. Self-reported height and weight (“How tall are you?”, 1 to 300 cm; “How much do you weigh, without clothes and shoes?”, 1 to 1000 kg; items chXXa016/chXXa017), combined as weight / height². The values the analysis reads are those of the frozen merged panel, which embeds construction-stage processing preserved in scripts/build_liss_merged.R: unit-error repairs (implausible heights and weights shifted by the offending factor, plus a handful of documented id-level corrections), person-level height smoothing (a respondent’s height set to their across-wave median or mean where reports varied), and filling of isolated missing inputs. The audit against the raw modules gives the footprint for waves 1 to 7: 87.3% of carried values match the raw computation exactly, 12.58% differ (49.6% of those by less than 0.5 kg/m², 41.9% by 0.5 to 2, and 8.5% by 2 or more, the unit-error repairs), and 0.11% were filled where the raw items yield no value (output/bmi_construction_audit_summary.csv, output/bmi_construction_audit_magnitudes.csv). A further 5,802 person-waves with a computable raw BMI are absent from the merged panel altogether (S2). No additional plausibility filter is applied at the analysis stage, and a robustness refit with every altered or filled value set missing reproduces the article’s conclusions (S5, S6; tables/bmi_sensitivity_*.csv). A reporting bias that is stable within persons is absorbed by the random intercept.

Physical activity. Weekly hours of sports participation behind a participation gate (“Do you practice sports?”; if yes, “How many hours do you spend on sports per week, on average?”, 0 to 168; items csXXa104/csXXa105); non-participants are zero hours. The construction record contains a person-mean imputation branch for participants with missing hours; the raw modules contain zero such cases in waves 1 to 7, so no analysed PA value is imputed. A plausibility ceiling is applied before the analysis subset so it propagates everywhere:

pa_ceiling <- 40
liss %<>% dplyr::mutate(pa = dplyr::if_else(pa > pa_ceiling, NA_real_, pa))

Values above forty hours per week exceed any plausible general-population sport load (the raw wave-1 maximum is 150) and are set missing, then handled by FIML like all other missingness.

Fruit and vegetable consumption. A two-item composite of the vegetable and fruit frequency items (“Do you eat raw or cooked vegetables?”, “Do you eat fruit?”, items chXXa196/chXXa197, six categories from “never” to “every day”), delivered pre-derived in the historical extract. The empirical mapping (output/frve_mapping.csv) is a sum-based cut of the two items: level 1 for a summed frequency of 8 or below, level 2 for 9 to 11, and level 3 for 12, so the top level means both vegetables and fruit eaten daily. Nine person-waves of 34,723 deviate from this rule, legacy anomalies of the archived extract (output/frve_mapping_deterministic.txt). Because the rule is not an item-wise recode, internal consistency is indexed on the two raw six-point items: pooled Spearman-Brown 0.428, per-wave 0.407 to 0.457 (output/frve_reliability_raw.csv, output/frve_reliability_pooled.csv).

The education-based SES strata

Education arrives as oplmet in the Background Variables (“Highest level of education with diploma”). The pipeline recodes it in two steps.

First, categories collapse to four levels: primary (with other and not yet started or completed assigned lowest), vmbo with havo/vwo, mbo, and hbo with wo:

educ = dplyr::case_when(
  oplmet == 1 ~ 1, oplmet == 2 ~ 2, oplmet == 3 ~ 2,
  oplmet == 4 ~ 3, oplmet == 5 ~ 4, oplmet == 6 ~ 4,
  oplmet == 7 ~ 1, oplmet == 8 ~ 1, oplmet == 9 ~ 1,
  TRUE ~ NA_real_
)

The frozen panel stores these codes as positive values 1 to 9, so the mapping operates exactly as written; 367 of the 5,676 analysis respondents reported an other or not (yet) completed or started category at their first observed wave (output/educ_other_count.txt).

Second, the household maximum is taken per wave (households with no observed education yield NA), and the four levels collapse to three by merging the two lowest, so the strata read: lower (up to general secondary), intermediate (mbo), higher (hbo or wo). The value at each respondent’s first observed wave defines the stratum, treated as time-invariant thereafter. Resulting sizes: 1,914 / 1,449 / 2,313, no missing values.

General secondary schooling is preparatory rather than qualifying in the Dutch system; as a highest completed level it indicates that no subsequent vocational or tertiary qualification was obtained, which is why it sits with the lower stratum.

Income equivalisation

Household net income is equivalised by dividing by the square root of a weighted household size, adults plus 0.8 times the number of children:

stand_inc = nethh / ((aantalhh - aantalki + 0.8 * aantalki)^0.5)

This is exactly lissr’s weighted_sqrt scale, and the pipeline asserts the identity at render time:

eq <- lissr::liss_equivalise_income(
  liss$nethh, liss$aantalhh, liss$aantalki, verbose = FALSE
)
ok <- is.finite(liss$stand_inc) & is.finite(eq)
stopifnot(isTRUE(all.equal(liss$stand_inc[ok], eq[ok])))

liss_equivalise_income() also offers scale = "oecd_modified" (divisor 1 + 0.5 * (adults - 1) + 0.3 * children) and scale = "sqrt" (square root of household size), which makes scale-choice robustness a one-argument change. Income does not define the SES strata and does not enter the reported models; the equivalised variable is retained for description and for possible income-based extensions.

Covariates

Gender (female = 1, from geslacht, coded 1 male and 2 female throughout the study window), age at each respondent’s first observed wave, and the medication count enter as time-invariant predictors of the random intercepts in the covariate-augmented model only; the focal estimates come from the unadjusted model. The medication count sums a reversed “I do not take any medicine” indicator and four category items from the health module’s “Are you currently taking medicine at least once a week for:” battery (high blood cholesterol, high blood pressure, heart or brain infarction, diabetes), range 0 to 5. Mokken scale analysis on the frozen extract supports the article’s threshold: pooled H = 0.721, per-wave H 0.702 to 0.730, no monotonicity violations (output/medication_mokken.csv, regenerable with make mokken). Smoking is excluded by design for the collider-bias reasons set out in the article’s Methods.
